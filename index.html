Abstract

We introduce a new egocentric dataset called Human Kitchen Interactions (HKI) to investigate the synth-to-real gap. Our dataset contains in total 100 synthetic and real videos in which 25 different actions are executed in a kitchen environment. The synthetic data is acquired in an egocentric virtual reality (VR) setup while capturing the virtual environment in a game engine.
